Statements that Explain the Change
Key Idea

A balance sheet provides a snapshot of an entity’s financial position on a specific date, showing what it owns (assets), owes (liabilities), and its net worth (equity). A balance sheet at the beginning of a period is often called the opening balance sheet. A balance sheet at the end of a period is often called the closing balance sheet.

To understand why the opening and closing balance sheets differ, we need additional statements—such as the cash flow statement, income statement, and statement of changes in equity—that explain what changed.
